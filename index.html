About the Development

Svev and SVIV are developed by musician, composer, and music educator Hans Martin Rundberg Austestad (2025)

Svev ‚Äì Background

SVEV was first created through work on recreating Eivind Groven's pure-tuning organ in an MPE version, making it possible to connect just intonation to VST libraries and MIDI sounds that many composers and performers already have on their computers. Earlier Groven apps exist, but have had limited usability and have become outdated. To make the apps easy to maintain, they are coded as standalone applications in the Python programming language. This should make them easy to maintain if system changes occur.

The new MPE technology, introduced in 2018, provides access to several useful functions. The app is developed to provide musicians, composers, educators, and researchers with a precise and intuitive tool for exploring harmonic contexts and tunings ‚Äì from natural harmonic just intonation to historical temperaments and experimental microtonality.

Sviv ‚Äì Background

SVIV started as a metronome that could handle asymmetric Norwegian time signatures. As new possibilities emerged during the development process, the app expanded to include several useful functions built on the same timing principle of replacing beats per minute with cycles per minute. Most groovy time signatures have the quality that different beats lag and push the measure.

SVIV should be useful for practice, performance, composition, analysis, and transcription. Now MIDI format can also play Telespringar that is readable and correctly notated.

Philosophy

Both projects emerge from Austestad's many years of experience as a musician, songwriter, and composer, and technological know-how. With particular attention to how existing tools often fall short when it comes to capturing the living and folk music aspects of intonation, rhythm, and expression.

It is important to emphasize that Austestad Apps are not commercial, there is no planned series of updates or new features in the near future. But if interest is strong, this may change. The apps will in any case be maintained if system changes require it.
