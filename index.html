Austestad Music Applications

Professional tools for musicians who need fine grained control in electronic production.

Real-time MIDI processor for dynamic microtuning. Works with MPE, historical temperaments, and custom tuning systems.

Rhythm and timing tool for elastic rhythms and asymmetric time signatures. Metronome with microtiming and sequencer.

About the Development

Svev and FOT are developed by musician, composer, and music educator Hans Martin Rundberg Austestad (2025).

Svev was first created through work on recreating Eivind Groven's pure-tuning organ in an MPE version. Further investigations revealed that earlier Groven apps had become outdated, and MPE technology could be utilized for several useful functions. The app is developed to provide musicians, composers, educators, and researchers with a precise and intuitive tool for exploring harmonic contexts and tunings ‚Äì from natural harmonic just intonation to historical temperaments and experimental microtonality.

FOT grew out of an attempt to create a metronome that could handle asymmetric Norwegian time signatures. As new possibilities emerged during the development process, the app expanded to include far more functions ‚Äì from practice to composition and analysis of rhythm and microtiming.

Both projects emerge from many years of experience as a musician, songwriter, and composer, with particular attention to how existing tools often fall short when it comes to capturing live music and folk music aspects of intonation, rhythm, and expression.
