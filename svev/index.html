About Svev

Explore a richer harmonic universe with real-time tuning.

Svev is a real-time application for dynamic microtuning based on MIDI Polyphonic Expression (MPE). In short, it functions as an intermediary between your MIDI instrument and audio software. It also allows you to retune your recorded midi performances.

When you play on a MIDI keyboard, Svev receives the signals, adjusts the intonation in real-time according to the settings you have chosen, and sends the adjusted signal to the sound-producing program ‚Äì for example Logic or Ableton Live.

Because Svev uses the MPE format, each note can be adjusted individually, not just the entire instrument simultaneously. This means you can explore finer nuances in intonation ‚Äì for example by using:

  • Auto-tuning modes that automatically correct pitch,
  • Historical temperaments (such as pure tuning or meantone temperament),
  • or custom tuning systems you create yourself.

For this to work, the audio software or synth you use must support MPE, which was first introduced in 2018. Many modern DAWs and VST instruments now have built-in support for this. Svev uses this capability to introduce microtuning in several ways.

Applications

Performers

Svev functions as an ear-training tool that lets you experience and control small differences in intonation in real-time. You can practice with a sophisticated tuner that continuously updates according to the selected temperament, and investigate how microscopic variations affect timbre and consonance.

This makes Svev an ideal tool for sharpening intonation awareness and developing precision control ‚Äì whether you play, sing, or combine this with MIDI control. This way you can work on fine-tuning your own intonation and get direct, auditory feedback on how small adjustments change the sound.

Demo from tuner window

Composers

Svev opens up composition in flexible and living tuning systems, where you can shape new harmonic spaces and flowing moods. You can program your own microtunings, combine different temperaments, and create music that dynamically shifts between tonal landscapes. 3 different auto-tuning modes can also be used to breathe life into otherwise sterile midi recordings, giving them a more organic and natural sound.

As a MIDI filter, Svev can be used before, between, and after other MIDI software. For example, you can send MIDI from Dorico through Svev and into Logic to get pure-intonation playback from your notation.

Svev also lets you change microtuning in real-time while playing, so you can fine-tune the intonation until you get exactly the harmonic expression you want.

When transcribing older recordings, you can adjust the concert pitch at decimal level, or use the built-in audio analysis function to reconstruct realistic temperaments ‚Äì for example when arranging old folk tunes.

Demo from fullscreen mode

Educators

Svev makes teaching intonation, temperament, and acoustics concrete and interactive. Students can see and hear how small frequency changes affect intervals and overtones, and thus gain an intuitive understanding of how different tuning systems work.

Do you have high school students who need to understand the harmonic series and the difference between this and Wohltemperierung? Or a singer who should learn to sing with pure natural tones? Then Svev can be a particularly useful supplement to your teaching ‚Äî a tool that makes abstract sound phenomena visible, audible, and measurable.

Researchers

Svev is a flexible and precise tool for documentation and analysis of temperament. The app shows both concert pitch in hertz, cent deviation for each scale degree, and exact frequency per note.

This makes it possible to visualize and compare intonation practices in a precise and interactive way, for example by studying differences between various fiddle traditions or vocal traditions.

Svev can be used both as an analysis tool and as an experimental platform for testing theoretical models of intonation and perception.

Institutions

Svev is an interdisciplinary learning tool that connects music, technology, mathematics, acoustics, and programming.

The app can be used in project-based teaching, where students and researchers collaborate to explore sound, frequency, and the connection between theory and practice.

In other words, Svev creates an arena where performers, composers, educators, and researchers can meet through a common, precise, and flexible tool.

Features and Tools

Main Window

Demo from main window

  • Keys: Color-coded to show intonation deviation from 12-tone equal temperament (12-TET). Colors change dynamically to indicate whether the note is low (blue), high (red), or precise (gold) relative to standard temperament.
  • Sliders: Each note has its own color-coded intonation slider that displays and adjusts deviation from 12-TET. You can manually fine-tune each note and hear the result in real-time.
  • Active note panel: Displays real-time information for the active note: note name, cent deviation from standard temperament, exact frequency in Hz, and MIDI velocity (attack strength).
  • Concert pitch box: Shows the current concert pitch (A-frequency) with up to two decimal precision. Is used as a reference point for all manual or automatic tuning.

Tuner Window

Demo from tuner

  • Pitch meter: Shows active frequencies in real-time, adjusted according to selected temperament. Provides an overview of the intonation of active notes ‚Äì like a dynamic digital tuner.
  • Graph window: Draws an intonation curve with color coding: blue = low (flat), gold = precise, red = high (sharp). The graph updates continuously and provides a visual picture of how stable the intonation is over time.
  • Vertical keyboard: Shows the same notes as the graph and reflects intonation visually. Keys are clickable and can be played as reference tones for comparison.
  • Ear icon: Activates or deactivates the listening function for pitch meter and graph. When listening is active, Svev analyzes incoming sound and updates visual intonation in real-time. When listening is deactivated, the graph freezes, allowing you to study the measurements without further updates.
  • Graph range: Lets you limit the pitch range to be displayed. By focusing on fewer notes, you get a larger and more detailed visual representation.
  • Suggest intonation: Analyzes graph data and automatically suggests new temperament based on measured pitches.
  • Eraser: Resets or clears the graph, allowing you to start a new measurement cycle.

  • Supports Norwegian Nynorsk and English, with language selection in settings.
  • Full support for MIDI Polyphonic Expression (MPE) ‚Äì each note can be adjusted individually (requires MPE-compatible synth).

  • Preset system for saving and reusing custom temperaments, tonics, and settings.
  • System for hotkeys and MIDI assignment, allowing you to switch temperaments and configurations directly from your controller.

  • Real-time tuner with graphical display of cent deviation and timbre curve.
  • Tone-trace graph showing pitch variations over time.
