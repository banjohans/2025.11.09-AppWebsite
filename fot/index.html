About Fot

An elastic metronome unlike any other. This one can create swinging grooves for you who practice more than just keeping the beat.

FOT is a rhythm and timing tool for musicians working with living rhythms ‚Äì not just straight lines. The app handles crooked time signatures like Pols, Polska, and Springar, or you can quickly set up a rhythm exactly as you wish.

You can use FOT as a standalone metronome, or connect it to MIDI and use it as a beat machine and sequencer ‚Äì with its own accents, tones, subdivisions, and timing.

Instead of fixed BPM beats, FOT works with cycles where each beat can have its own length. Everything is flexible, and can give you insight into what swings because of or despite not being straight.

Multiple instances can run simultaneously, in sync or at their own tempo. This lets you program both metrical and free rhythms and polyrhythms. The interface is intuitive and visual ‚Äì you can calculate your way, or feel your way, to the rhythm you want.

Applications

Performers

FOT is a practice tool for musicians working to understand and play rhythms where the microstructure is not straightforward ‚Äì such as in Pols, Polska, Springar, and other living folk dance forms. You can program in the actual time shifts in the beats, and with the sequencer you can create sequences that swing in different ways.

This allows you to train timing consistency, accent placement, and rhythmic precision in a more authentic way than with a regular metronome. By using the metronome together with the sequencer, you can bring the metronome to live performances.

Ensemble / Collaboration / Dance

FOT can run multiple metronomes simultaneously, sending signals to different MIDI outputs with different rhythmic patterns and/or tempos. This opens up for advanced patterns, but also for collaboration and interaction where performers can be in different time zones ‚Äì in their own time, yet still in a common cycle.

With both audio signals and visual indicators, FOT can help participants interact with and without sound. And thus, one can use the app for theater, movement, and sound in new ways.

Improvisers

For those working with improvisation, FOT is a tool for creating flexible and organic time frames. You can build patterns where core pulse can vary freely, or test alternative internal proportions in real-time.

With FOT you can also create extreme rhythmic landscapes with many asymmetrically subdivided beats in slow and fast tempos.

Composers and arrangers

FOT lets you build polymetric or asymmetric time signatures by adjusting percentage distribution and subdivisions per beat. You can create complex rhythm sequences, experiment with microscopic time shifts, and shape grooves that go beyond the standardized grid in a DAW.

When you're satisfied with the result, you can export MIDI data directly to your production environment, or take it further to notation software for transcription and arrangement. This makes FOT a compositional tool that bridges the gap between living rhythmic expression and digital production.

FOT can also be used to analyze existing music: Tap in the rhythm from a recording, and FOT will calculate microtiming, CPM, and percentage distribution ‚Äì so you can reconstruct and notate complex rhythms with precision.

Music educators

FOT makes teaching rhythm, timing, and groove concrete and interactive. You can demonstrate how living time signatures deviate from even quarter pulse, visualize percentage distribution in asymmetric time signatures, and give students tangible explanations of what makes a rhythm uneven.

With FOT students can see, hear, and measure differences in timing ‚Äì and gain a deeper understanding of how microtiming affects groove and musical expression. The app can be used in both individual teaching and group settings, and makes abstract concepts like percentage distribution, polyrhythms, and CPM accessible and understandable.

Researchers and analysts

FOT collects structured timing data such as cycles per minute (CPM), average measure time, and percentage per beat. This makes it possible to analyze and compare rhythmic practices across styles, epochs, traditions, and performers.

With FOT you can document and visualize differences in rhythmic execution, investigate stability and consistency in tapping over time, and obtain demonstrable data on how microtiming varies between different musical contexts.

FOT can thus be used as an analytical tool for ethnomusicology, rhythm research, and music psychology ‚Äì or as a way to explore personal musical traits of individual performers.

Features and Tools

Main Window

The main window in FOT consists of an interactive metronome wheel with visual beat cards, where you can shape your rhythm directly through intuitive controls.

  • Metronome wheel (circular display): A visual representation of your cycle, where each beat is shown as a pie piece. The size of the pie piece shows how much time the beat gets of the total. You can drag the pie pieces to adjust percentage distribution, and click on them to change subdivisions.
  • Beat cards: Visual modular "cards" for each beat, with slider for weight/percentage, subdivision counter, and MIDI parameters. You can enter values directly or drag the sliders to adjust. Each beat can get its own pitch for use with external synths.
  • CPM (Cycles Per Minute): Shows how many complete cycles (measures) are played per minute. This provides a more precise tempo measurement than traditional BPM when working with asymmetric rhythms.
  • Percentage distribution: Each beat can get a portion of total measure time, shown as percentage. This makes it possible to model uneven internal structure ‚Äì so you can create rhythms with natural "swing" or asymmetric pulses.
  • Subdivision per beat: Independent control of subdivisions (up to 16 per beat) for individual beats. This lets you create complex rhythmic structures with varying levels of detail.
  • Weight and sound: Separate volume for master beats and subdivisions, with WAV-based click sound (click.wav). You can open the sound panel (W) to adjust volume and sound character.

Sequencer and Tap-Tempo

FOT has a built-in sequencer that lets you record and save rhythm sequences, plus a precise tap-tempo tool for capturing living timing.

  • 9 separate measure loops: Record and save up to 9 different rhythmic loops, with ability to add and shape bass lines per loop. This makes it possible to build complex rhythmic arrangements.
  • 1-shot or loop queue system: Set up a sequence of your pre-saved loops, and play them in sequence or as a continuous loop. With hotkeys (1-9) you can add and edit your queues like a loop station.
  • Precise tap-tempo: A dedicated tap tempo window captures exact time per beat when you tap in the rhythm. FOT calculates the average of the rhythm you tap in so you can load this directly into the metronome. This is perfect for mimicking rhythms from recordings, or for capturing your own ideal measure swing.
  • MIDI export: Export a long series of tapped beats as MIDI file and use them in DAWs or notation software. This function lets you, for example, import a beat map of a song recorded freely without click, and still be able to transcribe the recordings with what will look like regular notation.

Presets from world rhythms, and possibility for saving and mass import

FOT comes with a bundle of ready-made presets for rhythms within Norwegian folk music and dance, and a small selection of rhythms from Europe. Furthermore, it lets you save your own measure structures, with the possibility of folder import that opens many metronomes at once. You will also be able to share your presets with others.

  • Export / import of measure presets: Save cycle data (metronome_data) and retrieve later through the Save / Import menu. You can also share presets with other musicians or use them across projects.
  • Storage in directories: Organize measure structures in folders (lagratakter, Metronomes) for easy reuse and sharing. It is possible to mass import entire directories with measure presets, which will open multiple metronomes simultaneously.

Multiple Instances in Sync

FOT can run multiple metronomes simultaneously, each with its own pattern, tempo, and color theme ‚Äì but with synchronized start and stop.

  • Unlimited instances: Run as many metronomes as you need simultaneously, each with its own color theme and visual identity.
  • Synchronization: Choose whether instances should have synchronized tempo, or run with individual tempos. You can also choose whether they should start and stop simultaneously, or have independent control.
  • Polyrhythms: Build complex polyrhythmic structures by combining multiple metronomes with different cycle lengths or tempos. This makes it possible to practice advanced rhythmic relationships.

Visual Interface

FOT has multiple display modes and additional panels to give you full control and overview of the rhythm.

  • Full view and Simple view: Switch between full functionality and a simplified display, so you can work with multiple windows simultaneously without losing overview. The views have an elastic wheel that adapts to screen size, and lets you adjust rhythms by drag and drop.
  • EKG/Pulse graph: Pulse panel that shows the rhythm as a continuous "pulse" curve ‚Äì similar to an EKG. This provides a visual representation of rhythmic dynamics and timing.
  • Keyboard panel: Keyboard panel lets you select or record MIDI pitches for each beat. Useful when using FOT as sequencer or beat machine.
  • MeasureTapper (tap-tempo window): Dedicated side window with data collection of average percentage, CPM, and average measure time. Provides detailed feedback on rhythmic stability and consistency.

Data Collection and Analysis

FOT collects structured timing data that can be used for analysis and documentation of rhythmic practice.

  • Percentage distribution per beat: MeasureTapper calculates average percentage for each beat based on what you tap in. This shows how much time each beat gets on average.
  • CPM (Cycles Per Minute): Shows how many complete cycles are played per minute, and provides a precise measure of tempo.
  • Average measure time: Shows how long a complete cycle takes on average, measured in seconds or milliseconds.
  • Stability and consistency: Can be used to monitor stability and consistency in tapping over time, and provides demonstrable data on microtiming variations.
  • Thread-safe mirroring: Internal mirroring of slider values and subdivisions for thread-safe playback without clicks or delays.

Core Functions

  • Full MIDI integration: Use FOT as intermediary between MIDI instrument and DAW, VST synths, or external hardware.
  • WAV-based click sound with adjustable volume and tone character.
  • Quick access to functions through keyboard shortcuts: W (sound panel), G (EKG graph), K (keyboard), T (Telespringar), V (Valdresspringar).
  • Multilingual support: Norwegian Nynorsk and English, with language selection in settings.
  • Visual feedback: Color coding of beats and subdivisions to make rhythmic structure easier to understand.

With FOT you can document and visualize differences in rhythmic execution, investigate stability and consistency in tapping over time, and obtain demonstrable data on how microtiming varies between different musical contexts.

FOT can thus be used as an analytical tool for ethnomusicology, rhythm research, and music psychology ‚Äì or as a way to explore personal musical traits of individual performers.

Features and Tools

Main Window

The main window in FOT consists of an interactive metronome wheel with visual beat cards, where you can shape your rhythm directly through intuitive controls.

  • Metronome wheel (circular display): A visual representation of your cycle, where each beat is shown as a pie piece. The size of the pie piece shows how much time the beat gets of the total. You can drag the pie pieces to adjust percentage distribution, and click on them to change subdivisions.
  • Beat cards: Visual modular "cards" for each beat, with slider for weight/percentage, subdivision counter, and MIDI parameters. You can enter values directly or drag the sliders to adjust. Each beat can get its own pitch for use with external synths.
  • CPM (Cycles Per Minute): Shows how many complete cycles (measures) are played per minute. This provides a more precise tempo measurement than traditional BPM when working with asymmetric rhythms.
  • Percentage distribution: Each beat can get a portion of total measure time, shown as percentage. This makes it possible to model uneven internal structure ‚Äì so you can create rhythms with natural "swing" or asymmetric pulses.
  • Subdivision per beat: Independent control of subdivisions (up to 16 per beat) for individual beats. This lets you create complex rhythmic structures with varying levels of detail.
  • Weight and sound: Separate volume for master beats and subdivisions, with WAV-based click sound (click.wav). You can open the sound panel (W) to adjust volume and sound character.

Sequencer and Tap-Tempo

FOT has a built-in sequencer that lets you record and save rhythm sequences, plus a precise tap-tempo tool for capturing living timing.

  • 9 separate measure loops: Record and save up to 9 different rhythmic loops, with ability to add and shape bass lines per loop. This makes it possible to build complex rhythmic arrangements.
  • Precise tap-tempo: Captures exact swing and microtiming when you tap in the rhythm. FOT calculates the average of what you tap and lets you load this directly into the metronome. This is perfect for reproducing rhythms from recordings or live performances.
  • MIDI export: Export tapped beats as MIDI file and use them in DAWs or notation software to arrange and make music with the right swing. Or beatmap music that was made without a click ‚Äì so you can synchronize existing recordings to a rhythmic grid.

Presets of folk and world-rhythms, with Storage and Bulk Import

FOT comes with ready-made presets for Norwegian folk dances, and lets you save and share your own measure structures.

  • Ready-made folk dance setups: Telespringar (T) and Valdresspringar (V) load predefined internal proportions and accents ‚Äì based on field recordings and ethnomusicological research. This makes it easy to practice authentic folk dance rhythms.
  • Export / import of measure presets: Save cycle data (metronome_data) and retrieve later through the Save / Import menu. You can also share presets with other musicians or use them across projects.
  • Storage in directories: Organize measure structures in folders (lagratakter, Metronomes) for easy reuse and sharing.

Multiple Instances in Sync

FOT can run multiple metronomes simultaneously, each with its own pattern, tempo, and color theme ‚Äì but with synchronized start and stop.

  • Unlimited instances: Run as many metronomes as you need simultaneously, each with its own color theme and visual identity.
  • Synchronization: Choose whether instances should have synchronized tempo, or run with individual tempos. You can also choose whether they should start and stop simultaneously, or have independent control.
  • Polyrhythms: Build complex polyrhythmic structures by combining multiple metronomes with different cycle lengths or tempos. This makes it possible to practice advanced rhythmic relationships.

Visual Interface

FOT has multiple display modes and additional panels to give you full control and overview of the rhythm.

  • Full vs Simple view: Switch between full functionality and a simplified display, so you can work with multiple windows simultaneously without losing overview.
  • EKG/Pulse graph: Dedicated panel (G) that shows the rhythm as a continuous "pulse" curve ‚Äì similar to an EKG. This provides a visual representation of rhythmic dynamics and timing.
  • Keyboard panel: Shortcut (K) for selecting MIDI pitches for each beat. Useful when using FOT as sequencer or beat machine.
  • MeasureTapper (tap-tempo window): Dedicated side window with data collection of average percentage, CPM, and average measure time. Provides detailed feedback on rhythmic stability and consistency.

Data Collection and Analysis

FOT collects structured timing data that can be used for analysis and documentation of rhythmic practice.

  • Percentage distribution per beat: MeasureTapper calculates average percentage for each beat based on what you tap in. This shows how much time each beat gets on average.
  • CPM (Cycles Per Minute): Shows how many complete cycles are played per minute, and provides a precise measure of tempo.
  • Average measure time: Shows how long a complete cycle takes on average, measured in seconds or milliseconds.
  • Stability and consistency: Can be used to monitor stability and consistency in tapping over time, and provides demonstrable data on microtiming variations.
  • Thread-safe mirroring: Internal mirroring of slider values and subdivisions for thread-safe playback without clicks or delays.

Core Functions

  • Full MIDI integration: Use FOT as intermediary between MIDI instrument and DAW, VST synths, or external hardware.
  • WAV-based click sound with adjustable volume and tone character.
  • Quick access to functions through keyboard shortcuts: W (sound panel), G (EKG graph), K (keyboard), T (Telespringar), V (Valdresspringar).
  • Multilingual support: Norwegian Nynorsk and English, with language selection in settings.
  • Visual feedback: Color coding of beats and subdivisions to make rhythmic structure easier to understand.
