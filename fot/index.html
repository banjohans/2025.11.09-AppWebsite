About Fot

An adaptive metronome that masters elastic grooves, asymmetric time signatures, microtiming, and complex polyrhythms ‚Äì built for musicians who practice more than just keeping the beat.

FOT is a rhythm and timing tool made for musicians who work with living rhythms ‚Äì not just straight lines. The app handles Pols, Polska, Springar, and other crooked time signatures with ease.

You can use FOT as a standalone metronome, or connect it to MIDI and use it as a beat machine and sequencer ‚Äì with its own weights, tones, subdivisions, and timing. Everything stays flexible.

Instead of fixed BPM beats, FOT works with cycles where each beat can have its own length. You can shape micro-shifts and accents the way they exist in all living music ‚Äì or create your own asymmetric pulse patterns.

Multiple instances can run simultaneously, in sync or at their own tempo. This lets you program both metrical and free polyrhythms. The interface is intuitive and visual ‚Äì you can calculate your way, or feel your way, to the rhythm you want.

Applications

  • Performers: Practice dance and folk music rhythms with uneven internal microstructure; or freely constructed rhythmic loops; train timing consistency and accent placement. Practice polyrhythmic patterns both metric and asymmetric. Use the metronome and sequencer as rhythmic foundation for live performance, and blend acoustic expressions with electronic ones.
  • Ensemble / Collaboration / Dance: Play and/or dance to different metronome instances, and find new ways to interact through the possibilities the app offers.
  • Improvisers: Create flexible patterns and time frames where core pulse can vary organically, and test alternative internal proportions in real-time. Create extreme rhythmic landscapes that can live freely or sync together.
  • Composers/Arrangers: Build polymetric or asymmetric time signatures by adjusting percentage distribution and subdivisions per beat. Create complex rhythm sequences, or take them further into your DAW for production, or export MIDI for further use in notation software for transcription or arranging.
  • Music Educators: Demonstrate how living time signatures can deviate from even quarter pulse; visualize percentage distribution in asymmetric time signatures, and gain access to concrete explanations of the unique characteristics of different time patterns.
  • Researchers/Analysts: Collect structured timing data (cycles per minute, average measure time, percentage per beat) for analysis, and gain easier access to demonstrable differences in various performance practices, epochs, styles, or personal musical traits of performers.
  • Teaching rhythmic precision: Interactively show how changing percentages, subdivisions, and weight affects groove experience and microtiming.

Features and Tools

Core Concepts

  • CPM instead of just BPM: "Cycles per minute" (CPM) as cyclic tempo unit in addition to classic beat feel
  • Percentage distribution per beat: Each beat within the cycle can get a portion of total measure time (average percentage) ‚Üí modeling uneven internal structure. Drag the pie pieces on the metronome wheel to adjust, and click on the pie pieces to change subdivisions
  • Beat cards: Visual modular "cards" for beats with slider (weight/percentage), subdivision counter, and MIDI parameters. Enter values directly or drag and drop in the sliders
  • Subdivision per beat: Independent control of subdivisions (up to 16 per beat) for individual beats
  • Weight and sound: Separate volume for master and subdivisions; WAV-based click (click.wav) with sound panel (W)
  • MIDI notes per beat: Each beat can be assigned pitch for use with external synths or pitch display
  • EKG/Pulse graph: Dedicated panel (G) for visual rhythmic "pulse"/curve
  • Keyboard panel: Shortcut (K) for MIDI tone selector

Sequencer and Tap-Tempo

  • Built-in sequencer: Record and save 9 separate measure loops, with the ability to add and shape bass lines per loop.
  • Precise tap-tempo: Captures exact swing (microtiming) when you tap in, calculates the average of what you tap, and lets you load this into the metronome.
  • MIDI export: Export tapped beats as MIDI map and use them in DAWs or notation software to arrange and make music with the right swing. Or, beatmap music that was made without a click.

  • Export / import of measure presets: Save cycle data (metronome_data) and retrieve later (Save / Import menu)
  • Store measure structures in directories (lagratakter, Metronomes) for reuse and sharing

Natural Time Flow

The metronome in FOT facilitates more musical and organic timing than a traditional click. Instead of completely even subdivisions, the time intervals between beats can be distributed unevenly, as often occurs in living music.

Customized Rhythm

You can adjust the distribution of time between beats, so that the rhythm gets its own pulse form or "swing". This makes it possible to practice the practical feel of groove, not just counting rhythm.

Folk Dance Presets

The program contains ready-made setups for springar measures and other (non-)traditional time patterns.

Independent Rhythm Streams

Multiple metronomes can run simultaneously, with their own patterns, but synchronized start and stop. This makes it possible to work with interplay, polyrhythms, and multi-layered rhythm ‚Äì and use different sounds per metronome via MIDI sends.

Visual Pulse Display

Each beat can be connected to sound, tone, or visual pulse. A graphical representation ‚Äì similar to an EKG ‚Äì shows the rhythm as a continuous line in motion, so you both see and hear the development.

Measurement and Feedback

The metronome can log how time usage is distributed between beats. This provides an objective basis for analyzing stability, precision, and personal rhythmic feel.
